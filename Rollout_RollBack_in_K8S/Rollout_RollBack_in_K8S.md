🚀 Kubernetes Rollout vs Rollback – Know the Difference! ☸️
If you're working with Kubernetes, understanding the difference between Rollout and Rollback is essential for safe application deployments.
🟢 🚀 Rollout
 ✅ Deploy a new version of your application
 ✅ Perform a rolling update with zero downtime
 ✅ Introduce new features and bug fixes
 ✅ Creates a new deployment revision

🟠 ⏪ Rollback
 ✅ Restore the previous stable version
 ✅ Recover quickly from failed deployments
 ✅ Minimize production impact
 ✅ Uses the previous deployment revision

💡 Simple Way to Remember
 ➡️ Rollout = Move Forward 🚀
 ⬅️ Rollback = Move Back 🔙

The best deployment strategy isn't about never making mistakes—it's about having a reliable way to recover when something goes wrong.
That's why Rollout and Rollback are two of the most important Kubernetes deployment operations.
